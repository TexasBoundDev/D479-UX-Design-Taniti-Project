Frequently Asked Questions

What language is spoken on Taniti?

Tanitian is the native language, but many residents also speak English, especially in tourist areas.

What currency is accepted?

U.S. currency is widely accepted throughout Taniti.

How can visitors get around the island?

Visitors can travel using rental cars, taxis, bicycles, and public transportation.

What activities are available on Taniti?

Visitors can enjoy beaches, hiking, surfing, cultural experiences, and local attractions.

Do I need a passport or visa to visit?

Visitors from most countries do not need a visa for short stays, but a valid passport is required. Check with your local embassy for specific requirements.

What is the best time to visit Taniti?

The best time to visit Taniti is during the dry season, which runs from December to April.

What is the legal drinking age on Taniti?

The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18 years old.

Is Taniti family friendly?

Yes, Taniti is a family-friendly destination with many activities and attractions suitable for all ages.

What is Taniti's biggest event celebration?

Taniti's largest annual celebration is the Festival of the First Sunrise, a week-long cultural event celebrating the island's heritage, community, and connection to the ocean.
